From Sign-Ups to Starters: Activating Micro-Creators Through AI-Guided Monetisation

Helping early-stage creators on Portaly move from passive sign-ups to their first revenue-generating action.

Role

Lead UX Designer

Timeline

4 Sprints

Platform

Web

Year

2023

Context

The Activation Gap

Portaly is a creator monetisation platform enabling income through digital products, live courses, donations, email capture, and 1-to-1 services. In November 2023, growth metrics revealed a clear problem: while creator sign-ups were increasing, monetisation behaviours were not keeping pace.

An "activated creator" was defined as someone who published at least one monetisable product or service within 14 days of sign-up. Most new users never reached this milestone.

48%

launched at least one monetisation method in the first month

60%+

completed profile setup but never configured pricing or paid offerings

0

structured pathways helping creators decide what to launch first

Creators weren't lacking tools. They were lacking clarity, confidence, and actionable first steps.

The Problem

The Monetisation Cold-Start Problem

Portaly offered multiple monetisation tools. But tools alone did not create action. When creators entered the monetisation page, they saw many possibilities but little guidance. Without a clear first step, hesitation followed.

Our high-level goals were to:

  • 01

    Lower the barrier to monetisation — help creators identify what they could realistically sell, even with small audiences or early-stage skills.

  • 02

    Turn vague ideas into actionable plans — provide structured, low-pressure steps that guide creators toward launching their first offering.

  • 03

    Build confidence through momentum — replace overwhelm with small, achievable progress that encourages continued engagement.

Discovery

Monetisation Feels Intimidating to Start

Our initial hypothesis was that creators needed better awareness of Portaly's monetisation features. Interviews quickly proved otherwise. Most early-stage creators were not blocked by missing tools — they were blocked at the moment of starting.

Many described monetisation as intimidating, high effort, and easy to abandon halfway. Even those with monetisation intent hesitated before launching anything.

"It takes too much time to make it look good… the pull isn't strong enough, so I gave up halfway."

"Selling makes me anxious. Promoting it through posts and stories over and over feels scary."

"I'm not confident pricing my work. I worry I'll charge too much."

The friction was not feature discovery. It was the first step.

Research Insights

Monetisation Needs a First Step, Not More Features

Combining product data with interviews across creator types uncovered not a lack of intent, but a consistent breakdown between ambition and action.

  • 01

    Creators did not lack ideas. They lacked clarity on what was realistic to launch first.

  • 02

    Monetisation felt like a big leap rather than a series of small steps.

  • 03

    Pricing triggered anxiety around self-worth and value positioning.

  • 04

    Time to set up felt disproportionately high compared to expected returns.

  • 05

    Creators preferred lightweight methods (donations, affiliate links) over building full products.

Early monetisation intent existed. Without guidance, it rarely translated into action.

Design Process

How We Got There

1. Product & Competitor Analysis

Competitive analysis revealed that most creator platforms were built to execute monetisation, not initiate it. They assumed creators already had an offer to sell — few provided guidance on what to launch first. Portaly shared this gap.

2. User Research

Through 9 in-depth creator interviews, we uncovered a consistent gap between monetisation intent and action. Creators expressed interest in earning, but hesitated at the point of committing to a specific offer.

Three behavioural themes emerged through affinity mapping:

  • —

    Ambition without structure — creators wanted to monetise, but lacked a clear first step.

  • —

    Perceived effort outweighed reward — setting up a paid offering felt like a significant commitment compared to uncertain returns.

  • —

    Confidence barriers — pricing, positioning, and self-promotion triggered hesitation and delay.

3. Reframing the Opportunity

Many creators already had valuable assets — content, skills, and small audiences. What they lacked was structure. We reframed the challenge around one question:

How might we help part-time creators turn what they already have into something they can launch this week?

4. Ideation Workshops

We explored multiple activation approaches — onboarding nudges, monetisation templates, pricing calculators, and gamified progress systems. Through cross-functional workshops and feasibility discussions with engineering, two evaluation criteria emerged: Would this reduce first-step ambiguity? Could this be implemented without rebuilding the monetisation system?

The breakthrough came when we shifted from "adding features" to "structuring decision-making" — leading to the concept of a conversational planning layer embedded directly within the monetisation page.

5. Journey Mapping

End-to-end journey mapping revealed that friction was concentrated at a single moment: when creators had to define their first offer. Most drop-offs occurred after users entered the monetisation page but before configuring any product or service. Rather than redesigning onboarding, we needed to support creators at the moment of decision.

Experiment

Structured Form vs Conversational Plan Builder

We were uncertain whether creators preferred a structured form or a conversational AI flow to generate their monetisation plan. I conducted a moderated usability A/B test with 10 creators on the monetisation entry page.

Version A — Structured Form

3 / 10 completed

Users filled out a multi-field form before generating their plan. Upfront articulation amplified uncertainty — a core pain point from research.

The Solution

Make Monetisation Feel Doable

We reduced the psychological and practical barriers to first-time monetisation by guiding creators to activate existing tools with clarity and confidence.

1. Guided Monetisation Portal

We introduced a dedicated monetisation entry point within Portaly — a conversational chat that narrowed options, matched creators with relevant monetisation paths, and reduced cognitive overload by limiting choices at each step.

2. Small-Step Activation Design

We broke monetisation into manageable, low-commitment tasks — turning vague ideas into simple action plans, designing step-by-step setup flows, and enabling progress anytime, even in short sessions.

Impact

Turning Sign-Ups into Monetising Creators

By introducing guided monetisation planning and breaking down complex goals into small, actionable steps, we helped early-stage creators move from passive sign-ups to active platform users.

52% → 84%

Users returning within first month to further set up or optimise their pages

48% → 56%

Users launching at least one monetisation method within first month

Rather than overwhelming users with monetisation possibilities, the experience focused on small commitments, contextual prompts, and achievable progress — making monetisation feel approachable instead of intimidating.

Reflection

Looking Forward

Beyond guided tools, we see an opportunity to build creator communities and mentor networks. Shared experience can accelerate confidence, experimentation, and sustained monetisation.

The key lesson: designing for activation is not about adding more features. It's about reducing the psychological weight of getting started.
